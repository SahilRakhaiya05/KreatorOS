# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

KreatorOS is built around professional, respectful collaboration. Contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and users are expected to keep discussions focused on product quality, correctness, security, maintainability, accessibility, and user value.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate clearly and assume good intent.
- Give technical feedback with specific examples and practical next steps.
- Respect different levels of experience, background, and perspective.
- Keep private credentials, customer data, workspace data, and sensitive screenshots out of issues, commits, prompts, fixtures, and logs.
- Raise security, privacy, payment, authentication, and AI-output concerns early.
- Preserve workspace, provider, billing, and human-approval boundaries during development.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Sharing secrets, credentials, tokens, private customer data, or sensitive workspace material.
- Bypassing auth, billing, provider, security, or AI approval boundaries for convenience.
- Introducing hidden data collection, hidden provider calls, or misleading product behavior.
- Deliberately disrupting project work, reviews, or discussions.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, reject contributions, restrict access, or take other reasonable action when behavior harms the project or its users.

Reports should include enough context for maintainers to understand the concern, such as links, screenshots with sensitive information redacted, affected files, or a short timeline of events.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, demos, and any official project communication space.
