# YC-grade product strategy

## The wedge

Creators do not want more dashboards. They want revenue outcomes.

The wedge is: **AI sets up and operates the first revenue workflow from one link.**

First magical demo:

1. Creator enters niche and goal.
2. AI creates a live page with a product, paid call, lead magnet, and brand inquiry.
3. Visitor books a paid call.
4. Payment, calendar, reminders, and customer record are created.
5. AI drafts follow-up and upsell.
6. Analytics shows conversion and suggests the next experiment.

## Why now

- AI tool calling makes app-native agents possible.
- Lower inference cost makes always-on operators feasible.
- Creator businesses are unbundling and rebundling.
- Brands need measurable creator campaigns.
- Scheduling, payments, messaging, and AI interviews are mature enough to compose.

## Avoid the trap

Do not build a huge feature list before one workflow is excellent.

The first product should dominate this loop:

`bio visitor -> qualified route -> paid booking/product purchase -> fulfillment -> follow-up -> second sale`

## Expansion path

1. Creator paid calls and products.
2. AI workflow automation.
3. Brand collaboration pipeline.
4. Research lab for offer/customer discovery.
5. Membership and course depth.
6. Brand discovery marketplace.
7. App/plugin ecosystem.

## North star metrics

- Time to first published monetized page.
- Time to first paid transaction.
- Percentage of creator revenue automated through workflows.
- Repeat purchase/book rate.
- Brand campaign completion rate.
- AI action approval rate.
- Workflow ROI.

## Defensible data moat

The platform learns from the creator business graph:

- Which offers convert by niche.
- Which routing questions predict buying intent.
- Which follow-ups drive second purchase.
- Which creator-brand collaborations complete successfully.
- Which customer interview themes predict new product revenue.

This is not generic chat data. It is monetization workflow data.
